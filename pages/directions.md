# {{page.title}}

Welcome to Digital Dramaturgy! This platform helps you publish annotated and edited playscripts for educational or dramaturgical purposes.

This page provides a very brief overview. For detailed instructions and comprehensive guidance, please **[visit our main Documentation site](/docs/)**.

## The Basic Workflow

Digital Dramaturgy generally follows these steps:

1.  **Prepare Your Script:**
    *   Use Google Sheets to create or adapt your playscript.
    *   You can use our [Shakespeare starter sheets]({{ site.baseurl }}/docs/quickstart.html#option-a-use-a-prepared-shakespeare-play) or [blank templates]({{ site.baseurl }}/docs/quickstart.html#option-b-prepare-your-own-script).
    *   Ensure your sheet has the [required columns](/docs/preparing-content.html#required-fields).
    *   **For full details, see: [`/docs/preparing-content.md`](/docs/preparing-content.html)**

2.  **Publish Your Google Sheet & Test (Optional):**
    *   Publish your Google Sheet to the web as a CSV file.
    *   You can then test its appearance on the main Digital Dramaturgy site by pasting your CSV link into the form below.
    *   **For full details, see: [`/docs/publishing-site.md`](/docs/publishing-site.html)**

    {% include annotate/setup/sheets.html %}

3.  **Create Your Own Site:**
    *   Fork our [Digital Dramaturgy GitHub repository](https://github.com/thecdil/dramabase).
    *   Configure it with your playscript CSV link.
    *   Deploy it using GitHub Pages.
    *   **For full details, see: [`/docs/publishing-site.md#part-3-setting-up-your-own-github-repository-and-deploying-via-github-pages`](/docs/publishing-site.html#part-3-setting-up-your-own-github-repository-and-deploying-via-github-pages)**
